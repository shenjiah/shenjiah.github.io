    The Triangular Relations Become History

    Posted at — Jun 7, 2002

    Taipei Times

    The Triangular Relations Become History By James Wang

    Without a fanafre,the United States and Russia reached a three-page long nuclear arms reduction treaty, and Russia becomes a partner of NATO, indicating its further integration with the West.

    After a bitter collapse in the early 1990’s, Russia finally finds its peace with itself and its former enemies.

    Now we can see a Russia that cooperates with the United States and other major powers to promote peace and stability around the world. We can see a Russia that walks away from confrontations and arms races and rushes toward economic development.

    Moscow understands that the future of Russia relied on accesses to the markets in the West and investments from overseas. The proud and nationalistic Russians are now satisfied to play a less significant role in international affairs.

    The Russians reoriented their policies toward privatization and free market. The United States has not yet called it free market economy. But, given time, the Russians are getting there under a democratic system.

    This is a historic evolution of Russia from the archenemy, politically, militarily, economically and ideologically, of the West for over half a century to a democratic partner.

    The United States has achieved one of its long standing policy gpals: to make Europe whole and free.

    Such developments would put a lot of pressure on the People Republic of China. When the fromer Soviet Union collapsed Chinese elder leader Deng Xiaoping was quoted as saying that it was worthy to kill twenty thousands innocent people to make sure the survival of the communist regime.

    Obviously the Chinese took a different approache to handle its domestic and foreign policy issues than the Russians. Beijing opened up its economy without carrying out any meaningful political reform. China’s developments were so imbalanced that the gap between the poors and the riches becomes one of the worst case in the world.

    The communist or socialist government was supposed to take better care of the welfare of the workers and farmers, the disadvantaged class of China, but in the name of better job and better living standard, the workers’ rights were sacrificed and farmers were left in poverty.

    Even insisting that it needs a stable international environment for its economic development, China, unlike Russia, has not found peace with its own people or its neighbors.

    China continues to build up military in the name of military modernization and bully Japan whenever Tokyo behaves to the dislike of Beijing.

    China vowed to swallow up Taiwan either by forces or by “peaceful means” and charged that the United States is the block to Beijing’s goal. China seems blind to Taiwan’s democracy or reluctant to face reality.

    Of course, the Chinese exploited their past humiliations and nationalism for good reason. But a regime that depends on nationalism for its survival is at a great risk. It could be pushed over the brink and faced a situation it may prefer to avoid.

    When Richard Nixon and Henry A. Kissinger seized the split of China and former Soviet Union to play so-called “China card” to serve the interests of the United States, China’s Mao and Zhou willingly played “America card” to fend off Soviets Union’s aggressive behavior and played “Russia card” to improve relations with the United States at the expenses of Taiwan.

    The so-called “Triangular Relations” lead to normalization of relations between the United States and China and detente between the United States and Soviet Union. It was credited with the ending of the Vietnam conflicts. The major victim of the big powers manipulation was Taiwan, which was under Chiang’s authoritarian rule and claimed to represent whole China, including Mongolia.

    China benefitted so much from playing “Russia card” that it tend to forget the “Russia card” no longer exist.

    Russia is so accommodating to the needs of the United States that it raised no objection to American use of military bases in former Soviet Republics. Moscow became a partner of NATO in the NATO-Russia Council and would let NATO expand to include former Soviet Republics.

    The American special forces are training troops in the former Soviet Republic of Georgia, an event that was unthinkable a few years ago.

    With the disappearence of the “Russia card,” it is time for China to adjust and reorient its domestic and foreign policies. The government should reconcil with its own people, begin long-over-due political reforms and respect human rights.

    It should respect Taiwan’s right to exist as a democratic and sovereign state if the people of Taiwan so desire.

    “It is economy, stupid,” said the slogan of former president Bill Clinton’s campaign. China’s future depends on its economic development and necessary political reforms, not acquisition of additional territories or bullying its neighbors.

    Its persistent military build up and threat to use forces against Taiwan could only ruin whatever it has achieved in economic front and lead China into another disaster.

    James Wang is a Washington-based journalist.
